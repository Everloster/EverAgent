# Chronobiology: Chronotypes & Circadian Phase

## Chronotype Definition
**Chronotype** describes individual preference for sleep-wake timing driven by circadian physiology, not behavior. Approximately **25% of humans are Evening Types (N3)**, with biological sleep midpoint (MSF) after 3 AM.

### Genetic Basis
- MCTQ (Munich ChronoType Questionnaire): 250,000+ subject dataset (Roenneberg et al., 2012)
- Core clock genes: CLOCK, PER3, CRY determine intrinsic period (~24.2–24.5h for evening types)
- Circadian period length → later chronotype

## Social Jet Lag (SJL)
**Definition**: Misalignment between biological clock and societal schedule. Morning-oriented jobs force early wake despite late intrinsic rhythm.

### Roenneberg's Core Finding (Current Biology, 2012)
| Variable | Early Type | Evening Type |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Intrinsic period | ~24.0h | ~24.3h |
| Sleep midpoint | ~03:00 | Post-03:00 |
| SJL from 9–5 job | Minimal | 1–2 hours |
| Metabolic risk (per 1h SJL) | Baseline | +33% obesity, elevated cortisol |

**Key insight**: Evening types in 9–5 cultures experience chronic sleep debt even with 8h sleep duration—circadian misalignment itself drives metabolic dysregulation.

## Hormone Phase Delay (Evening Types)
Relative to sleep onset, not clock time:

| Hormone | Onset after sleep | Peak after wake | Evening type shift |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Cortisol | 30–45 min | 8–9h post-wake | +2–3h later |
| Melatonin | Dusk−45min | (inverse) | Begins ~midnight |
| Growth hormone | ~90 min post-sleep | First pulse | ~3:30 AM |
| Testosterone | Early morning | (dawn peak) | Peak ~11 AM vs 7 AM |

## Practical Implication for Evening Types
- 11:30 PM training occurs at ~5–6 PM equivalent circadian time
- Body temperature, cortisol, alertness: still elevated relative to sleep need
- Natural sleep window: 2 AM–10 AM aligns with chronotype without forcing early wake
