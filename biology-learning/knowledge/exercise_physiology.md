# Exercise Physiology: Timing, Body Temp, Performance

## Core Body Temperature (CBT) & Strength
**Atkinson & Reilly (1996)** + **Chtourou & Souissi meta-analysis (113 studies)**:
- Muscle strength, power, anaerobic capacity peak within 2–4h post-CBT maximum
- General population: CBT max ~4–8 PM → strength peak ~4–8 PM
- Evening types: CBT max shifted ~2–4h later → strength peak ~6 PM–midnight

### Mechanism
Higher CBT → enzyme activity ↑, neural transmission speed ↑, muscle stiffness optimal → force generation +3–10% vs morning.

## Evening Type Training Window
For evening chronotypes (23:30 training):
- Core body temperature still elevated (due to phase delay)
- Circadian cortisol, alertness alignment: favorable
- **Within individual's strength peak window** (not sub-optimal)
- Performance metrics (force, velocity) expected to be high

## Training-Sleep Buffer Evidence
Post-exercise physiology requires cooling before sleep:
1. **Core temperature elevation** +1–2°C (persists 60–90 min post-training)
2. **Cortisol elevation** +50–100% (remains elevated 60–120 min)
3. **Sympathetic dominance** (elevated heart rate, catecholamines)
4. **Melatonin suppression** (high body temp + high cortisol = melatonin inhibition)

### Sleep entry requirements (opposite):
- Core temperature decreased
- Cortisol low
- Parasympathetic dominance
- Melatonin rising

**60 min buffer insufficient** if training intensity is high; **120–150 min optimal** (Czeisler lab work).

## Post-Training Sleep Quality Interventions

### Passive Cooling (most effective)
- **Warm shower (38–40°C, 10–15 min)**: Increases skin vasodilation → core heat dissipation (paradoxical but validated)
- Finish shower 30–40 min pre-bed for maximum effect
- Walker (2017): Meta-analysis of RCTs confirms warm bath/shower reduces sleep onset latency by 10–15 min post-exercise

### Active Nervous System Downregulation
- Stretching/foam rolling (not high-intensity):  ↓cortisol, ↑parasympathetic
- Avoid blue light 60 min pre-bed
- Temperature-controlled sleep environment (<18.5°C optimal)

### Nutrition Timing Adjustment
- "Anabolic window" myth: Protein synthesis elevated post-training window much wider than 30 min
- Safe to delay high-protein meal until 60–90 min post-training (calmer state, better digestion)
- Avoid large carb meals <90 min pre-bed (blood glucose fluctuation delays SWS entry)

## Chronotype-Specific Adaptation
**Thomas et al. (2020)**: Evening types undergoing evening training develop entrainment—the repeated 23:30 stimulus becomes a **Zeitgeber (time-giver)** that stabilizes circadian rhythm rather than disrupting it. Habituation possible after 4–6 weeks.
